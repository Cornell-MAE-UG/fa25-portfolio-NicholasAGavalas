MAE 3240 Portfolio Assignment

For this portfolio assignment, I chose to reflect on **Problem Set 4** from MAE 3240 Heat Transfer. This assignment was one of the most valuable for me because it tied together several of the main ideas from the course, especially conduction, convection, thermal resistance networks, and fin analysis. In particular, the fin problem made me work carefully through the relationships between fin efficiency, fin effectiveness, fin thermal resistance, and the geometric parameters that control heat dissipation. Rather than just plugging values into equations, I had to understand what assumptions were being made, such as steady-state behavior, one-dimensional conduction, and the use of a convection tip condition. Working through that process helped me better understand how the mathematics connects to the physical behavior of extended surfaces.

What made this homework especially useful was that it showed how heat transfer analysis can be used as a design tool. The problems were not only about finding temperatures, but also about evaluating whether a thermal design is actually effective. For example, in the fin analysis problem I had to determine how geometry and material properties affected the fin length, resistance, and effectiveness, which is directly relevant to engineering applications like heat sinks and thermal management systems. This assignment improved my confidence in building solution workflows for more complicated thermal systems and reinforced the importance of clearly organizing assumptions, governing equations, and final interpretations. I think that kind of structured heat transfer problem solving will be useful in future engineering work involving thermal design, energy systems, and mechanical components operating under temperature limits.
